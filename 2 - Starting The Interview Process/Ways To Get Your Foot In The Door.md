# Getting Your Foot In The Door

Here are the most effective ways of getting your foot through the door, from most to least effective

- Warm referrals (referrals from people you know and who have worked with you)
- In-house recruiting
- Applying online
- Cold outreach to hiring managers

And here are some ineffective ways:

- Cold outreach to recruiters
- Cold referrals (referrals from people who don’t know you)
- Agency recruiters contact you

## Applying Online

When it comes to applying online:

- It’s a numbers game. You need to apply a lot to just get a response from only a few companies
- Not all companies look at their online applications
- If a recruiter reaches out to you (in house), you are six to ten times more likely to secure the interview.

Be wary of agency recruiters.

## Cold Outreach

Cold outreach to recruiters doesn’t work. You’ll have more luck doing that with hiring managers at companies you want to join. The process will be described more in detail later in this document.

If you’re not the type of candidate that recruiters are reaching out to (experienced, senior, well-pedigreed), they're likely not to help you.

### Cold Outreach To Hiring Managers

Here is the process for handling cold outreach to hiring managers:

- Figure out what companies you’re into
    - Things to be mindful of when creating this list
        - Are there any dev tools you really admire?
        - Are there any open-source projects you follow from them?
        - Are there any engineers on social media you follow?
        - Look where your friends are working.
- Once you have that, construct your target list
    - Guidelines for reaching out
        - Managers
        - In position for less than five (ideally less than two) years
        - Close to where you want to work.
        - First or second degree connection
- Find their work emails and reach out to them there
- Remember, outreach and job applications are two different things. Here, you want to craft a succInct and personalized email to the manager. Make them want to reach back out to you
    - Here’s what you shouldnt do in these emails
        - Dont open with how you found them
        - Dont be overly formal. Usually first name is fine
        - Dont screw up pronouns / gender
        - Don’t paste a generic cover letter
        - Avoid buzzword soup
        - Don’t forget to attach a link to your personal page or LinkedIn profile or both
    - And here’s what should be in those emails:
        - Common ground with the target of your email
        - Proof that you're worthy of their time
            - Mention accomplishments
            - If you have it, use social proof (schools, former companies)
        - A call to action (like a call or a meeting).

## Timeline of the process

- 2 Days - Creating a target list
- 1 Day - Write your first draft email template
- 3 Days - Go through the target list and research who you'll be reaching out to (blog posts, open source contributions, etc.)
- 2 Days - Write outreach to the first batch
- 1 Week - Wait for results so you can iterate on the outreach
- 2 Days - Second batch
- 1 Week - Get results, iterate based off that
- 2 Days - Third batch
- 1 Week - Get results, iterate based off that
