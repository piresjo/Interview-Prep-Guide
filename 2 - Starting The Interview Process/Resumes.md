# Resumes

people look at resumes, but not much. So it’s worth optimizing, but no need to overoptimize

## What Recruiters Look At

With your resume, recruiters are more likely to reach out if

- You have niche skills
- You have prestigious work experience (like FAANG)
- You went to a prestigious school
- You’re part of an underrepresented group

Side projects are probably not necessary to add. It’s usually not a good idea to do projects just for the sake of job searching.

In short, recruiters don't read the resumes too closely, and per `interviewing.io`, "recruiters were only slightly better than a coin flip at identifying talent".

## What Makes An Adequite Resume

There are five steps to write a good resume

- Use a template
- Keep it to one or two pages
- Write up your work experience. Use clear concise bullets
- Add skills, education, and other sections
- Proofread

Here are some pointers for finding a good template

- Section for skills, experience, and education
- Way to show off easily where you worked (like having the workplaces) in a column
- Will allow you to fit everything in a page or two
- Reasonable white space
- Use bullets

### Work Experience

Identify highlights. Those should be closer to the top of the resume.

Bullet points should be

- Clear
- Concise
- Demonstrate what you did and its impact (when possible, use metrics)

Here's an example of a good bullet point:

> Created and launched a service that collects product opinions and recommendations from Twitter. The service finds related tweets, removes spam, analyzes sentiment and creates a structured database of everything that was said about particular products

Here's an example of a bad bullet point:

> Designed software application including: data modeling, software archetecture design, software-hardware integration, user interface design, and database management.
